# SaveKu — Client Brief

## Product

SaveKu Tabungan Mikro: a micro-savings feature inside the SaveKu fintech app. Users set a daily savings goal (as low as Rp 5,000/day) and the app auto-deducts from their e-wallet. Targets young professionals 22-32 who earn enough to save but don't have the habit yet.

## Brand Voice

Warm, relatable, slightly self-deprecating. SaveKu talks like a financially-aware friend, not a bank. Conversational Bahasa Indonesia mixed with casual English loan words where natural (the way urban millennials actually talk). Never preachy. Never condescending about spending habits.

## Target Emotions

Primary: "oh that's literally me" recognition → gentle motivation
Secondary: relief ("saving doesn't have to be painful"), curiosity ("wait, Rp 5,000 aja?")

## CTA Guidelines

Soft CTA only. Never "download now" or "sign up today." Preferred patterns:

- Ending with a question ("lu udah mulai belum?")
- Gentle suggestion ("coba aja dulu, Rp 5,000 doang")
- Social proof nudge ("temen gue udah nyoba dan dia bilang...")
  The CTA should feel like the end of a conversation, not an ad.

## Forbidden Words and Phrases

- "investasi" (too intimidating for the target audience)
- "financial freedom" or "kebebasan finansial" (overused, feels scammy)
- "passive income"
- "grind" / "hustle culture" framing
- Any comparison to crypto or trading
- "dijamin" or any guarantee language
- Direct competitor names

## Content Rules

- Never position saving as sacrifice. Frame it as something small and easy.
- Never shame spending. The audience spends on coffee and food — that's fine, the product works alongside those habits, not against them.
- Product mention should feel incidental, not central. The content is about the relatable situation first.
- All scripts should work as spoken word (TikTok/Reels narration style), not written captions.
